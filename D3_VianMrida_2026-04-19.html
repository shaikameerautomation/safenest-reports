Things to Confirm With Sales Team

Questions worth asking before you book

These are not warnings — they are things we found during due diligence that deserve a direct conversation with the developer before you sign anything.

This is the developer's first project
IRA VIAN PROPERTIES PRIVATE LIMITED has declared "no past experience" in its RERA filing. There are no previously completed projects to check for on-time delivery, construction quality, or how they handle post-possession issues. For some buyers, a first project by a professionally structured company with strong consultants and a clean legal setup is acceptable. For others, the absence of a track record is a firm "no."
What to do: Ask the developer who their construction contractor is, whether construction finance has been arranged, and request the names of their structural and MEP consultants. A 30-minute conversation with their project team will tell you a lot.
Land belongs to three individuals — revenue share arrangement
The land is owned by three separate people (P. Indira Devi, Mitesh Majithia, and Binita Anandpara) who have given a registered power of attorney to the developer in exchange for a share of revenue from unit sales. This is a common arrangement in Hyderabad, and all agreements are registered. The complexity is that all three must cooperate throughout the construction period — particularly at the time of individual flat registrations.
What to do: Ask the developer's legal team to confirm that all three landowners are parties to the Agreement of Sale proforma, and that the arrangement for flat registration is clearly documented. Your own property lawyer can review this at the AOS stage.
Clubhouse size — brochure and RERA figures differ
The brochure states 40,000 sft of clubhouse and amenities. RERA filings show indoor amenity area of approximately 23,840 sft across registered floors. The gap likely reflects outdoor terraces, stilt-level open zones, and amenity spaces counted differently. Neither figure is wrong — they may simply be measuring different things.
What to do: Ask the sales team for a floor-wise breakup of what is included in the 40,000 sft figure. Knowing what is indoor vs outdoor helps you plan how you will actually use the amenities.
100 ft road — proposed, not yet complete
The brochure highlights 100 ft road connectivity as a key feature. The project documents describe this as "HMDA Proposed & Approved." This road is planned but may not be complete when you move in. Current site access is via existing Kollur roads.
What to do: Visit the site and check the current road condition from the ORR service road to the project gate. Ask the developer for the HMDA timeline for this road's completion.

Common Questions

Questions buyers usually ask

Is the developer reliable if this is their first project?
There is no delivery track record to evaluate. What we can confirm: the company is registered under RERA, has a GST number, has appointed qualified architects and structural engineers, and has HMDA approval. The risk is real — we just cannot quantify it from available data. The best mitigation is to verify the developer's financial capacity and construction finance before booking.
What happens if they delay possession?
Under RERA, if the developer delays beyond the agreed possession date, they must pay you interest at SBI's MCLR rate + 2% per annum on your paid amount — for every month of delay. This applies automatically. You can also file a complaint with TSRERA. Make sure the Agreement of Sale states your possession date clearly as "36 months from Agreement of Sale date."
Is the price fair for what you get?
At ₹6,600/sft base rate in Kollur, with Mivan construction, 10 ft ceilings, and a 4-level sky clubhouse — the price-to-spec ratio is genuinely competitive compared to similar specifications at Kokapet (₹8,500–10,000/sft) or Financial District (₹12,000+/sft). The trade-off is construction risk with a new developer.
What is the maintenance cost after moving in?
Maintenance costs are not stated in available documents. With a 40,000 sft sky clubhouse and full amenities, expect higher maintenance than a standard project. Ask the developer for an estimated monthly maintenance charge per sft. Typically, premium projects in this belt run ₹3–5/sft/month.
Which banks have approved home loans here?
HDFC, Axis Bank, and Kotak Mahindra Bank have approved home loans. SBI, LIC Housing Finance, and Union Bank are listed as in process. Bank approval means these lenders have independently verified the project's legality — this is a positive signal beyond just the RERA registration.
